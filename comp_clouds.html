This plot reveals a direct correlation between cloudiness and latitude; thus, as the latitude increases, reaching northward, the cloudier the skies become. Despite the correlation seen between latitude and cloudiness in the Northern Hemisphere, the above plot indicates no such correlation exists in the southern hemisphere. If anything, this plot shows the southern latitudes are more likely to have either clear skies or complete cloud cover, as most data points are confined to 0% and 100% data values.
